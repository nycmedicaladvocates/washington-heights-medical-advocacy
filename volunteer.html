VOLUNTEER

Help New Yorkers navigate healthcare

Volunteers provide practical support: finding the right place to start, preparing questions, organizing documents, and connecting people to reputable services.

Free medical advocacy and navigation. No cost.
See how we help Read about the program Privacy

What volunteers do

  • Clarify the goal and define the next step
  • Find reputable resources and correct contact points
  • Help prepare a short call script and question list
  • Help organize documents and timelines
  • Follow up and track progress

What volunteers need

  • Clear communication and reliability
  • Comfort with phone calls and forms
  • Respect for privacy and boundaries
  • Commitment to using reputable sources
Volunteer standards
  • Use official or reputable sources whenever possible
  • Document help in brief notes for continuity
  • Do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations
  • Escalate urgent safety situations to emergency services and crisis support resources

Safety and disclaimer
In an emergency, call 911. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988. This site provides general information and free navigation support. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
